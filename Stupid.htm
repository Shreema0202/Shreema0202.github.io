Myth 1: The people living in the Middle Ages believed that the Earth was flat.

Truth 1: Apparently scholars have known that the earth was round for thousands of years. This theory was first put forward by a Greek philosopher in 500 B.C. I mean come on, do you really think our ancestors were that dumb?


Myth 2: After your death, your nails and hair continue to grow.

Truth 2: For some reason, many people think this is true, but if you use common sense, it's pretty apparent that this is a kind of stupid myth. After you die, like the rest of your body, your nails and hair also start to decay because there is nothing keeping them alive.


Myth 3: The 5 second rule.

Truth 3: Many people use this so called 'rule' to eat stuff that they've accidentally dropped onto the bacteria-infested ground. In reality, it takes a second, if even that, for heaps of harmful bacteria to get onto your food. So, if you're one of those people who use this rule as an excuse, I would strongly suggest you stop.



Myth 4: Van Gogh cut off his ear for a lover

Truth 4: While Gogh did cut off his ear, it was only a small piece of his ear lobe - and it wasn't for a lover either. He cut it off during a violent spat with Paul Gaugin (another famous artist). Whether or not Gogh gave it to a local girl still remains a mystery.



Myth 5: Slaves built the 'Pyramids'

Truth 5: Egyptologists say this ancient construction job was a great honour granted to only respected labourers, who to this day lay entombed nearby.




Myth 6: Walt Disney drew the first Mickey Mouse

Truth 6: Nope. The first Mickey Mouse was actually drawn by the company's chief animator, Ub Iwerks and for some reason the credit went to Disney.
